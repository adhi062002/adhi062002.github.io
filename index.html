Multi-Sensor Fusion for Autonomous UGV Localization
CSIR-National Aerospace Laboratories
Integrated visual-inertial odometry (VINS-Fusion) with 2D LiDAR and RGB-D sensing for GPS-denied navigation. Designed and implemented an error-state Extended Kalman Filter for pose fusion, with LiDAR-based pose correction and depth projection. Sensor synchronization and coordinate frame alignment handled using ROS2 TF. Evaluated trajectory performance using Absolute Trajectory Error (ATE) and Relative Pose Error (RPE), with architecture structured for future migration to factor-graph optimization.

022024 – present
YOLO-Based Real-Time Dent Detection System
CSIR-National Aerospace Laboratories
Built real-time defect detection pipeline for aircraft surface inspection using YOLO (v5/v8) integrated with robotic sensing. Developed a dual-model YOLOv8 framework combining a dent detector and a rivet detector via cross-model Non-Maximum Suppression — eliminating cross-class confusion and achieving ~88% overall detection accuracy. Also developed a Modified YOLOv8 pipeline with CLAHE, Laplacian edge enhancement, and HSV normalization that reached 96.11% accuracy at confidence 0.3. Validated across composite, metal, and fibre metal laminate specimens under varied lighting and environmental conditions.

UGV Design for Aircraft Structure Inspection
CSIR-National Aerospace Laboratories
Designed and iterated two mechanical versions of an Unmanned Ground Vehicle for automated aircraft surface scanning, supporting a 3D scanner payload (~3 kg). Resolved stability issues from the initial design — caused by an overhanging scanner mount — by centralizing the camera and adopting a simplified stand architecture with servo-controlled yaw and pitch. Developed ROS2 packages for servo control, motor drive, and image processing pipelines running on Raspberry Pi with remote laptop communication.
